Ocated in the vibrant heart of Pemba, Mozambique, Poporos is nestled in a prime corner location, offering stunning views and easy access to the city’s bustling life. Just a stone’s throw from the scenic coastline, this restaurant embodies the charm and elegance of a bygone era, combining a sophisticated ambiance with a modern touch. Designed with an intricate and ornamental style, Poporos evokes a sense of timeless beauty, bringing a unique and inviting atmosphere to Pemba’s most sought-after dining district.

Typical Mozambican Food Menu 🇲🇿🍽️

Starters

  • Chamussas – Mozambican samosas filled with spicy meat or vegetables. 200 Mt
  • Rissóis de Camarão – Shrimp turnovers, crispy on the outside, creamy on the inside. 290 Mt
  • Salada de Abacate e Camarão – Fresh avocado and shrimp salad with a citrus dressing. 365 Mt

Main Dishes

  • Matapa – A rich dish made from cassava leaves, peanuts, coconut milk, and sometimes shrimp or crab. 570 Mt
  • Caril de Camarão – Mozambican-style prawn curry, usually served with rice. 850 Mt
  • Frango à Zambeziana – Grilled chicken marinated with coconut, garlic, and lime, a specialty from the Zambezia region. 1200 Mt
  • Arroz de Coco com Peixe Grelhado – Coconut rice served with grilled fish, often barracuda or grouper. 920 Mt
  • Feijão Nhemba – Cowpea beans cooked with coconut milk and served with rice or xima. 480 Mt

Side Dishes

  • Xima – Traditional maize porridge, a staple in Mozambican cuisine. 180 Mt
  • Salada de Feijão Catarino – A refreshing bean salad with onions, tomatoes, and vinegar. 630 Mt
  • Frita – Fried cassava, crispy on the outside, soft inside. 370 Mt

Desserts

  • Bolo Polana – A cashew and potato cake, one of Mozambique’s most famous desserts. 390 Mt
  • Cocada – A sweet coconut candy, chewy and delicious. 266 Mt
  • Pudim de Leite – Mozambican-style caramel flan. 468 Mt

Drinks

  • Sumo de Cajú – Fresh cashew juice, a local favorite. 170 Mt
  • Laurentina Preta – A popular Mozambican dark beer. 190 Mt
  • Pombe – A traditional fermented drink made from maize or cassava. 170 mt
  • Laurentina Clara – A popular Mozambican dark beer. 190 Mt
  • Tipo Tinto – A famous locally produced rum. 150 Mt
